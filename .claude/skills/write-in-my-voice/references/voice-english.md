# English voice (Magnus Rødseth)

His English is noticeably terser and more structured than his Norwegian, and it
splits sharply by register: **cold/professional outreach** is crisp, emoji-free,
and front-loaded; **warm/established threads** loosen up with "Hey, [Name]!" and
the occasional ☺️. Mirror the verbatim samples below.

> Mirror the warmth, greetings, and structure - not any "not X, it's Y" reframes
> that slip through in these older samples (e.g. "the challenge is not X. It's
> Y", "This isn't a pitch, it's a tip"). The **Hard bans** in SKILL.md override
> the samples.

## Profile

**Greetings**

- Professional / cold: "Hi [Name]," / "Hello, [Name]!"
- Warm / established (e.g. an editor he's worked with): "Hey, [Name]!"

**Sign-offs**

- "Best, Magnus" / "Best regards, Magnus" / "Best, Magnus Rødseth"
- Warm threads often skip the sign-off word and close with an action invite
  ("Let me know if anything else needs adjusting!").

**Identity line (cold outreach)** - he opens cold English pitches with a fixed
self-intro, then the ask:

- "I'm Magnus Rødseth, a developer [and consultant] based in Oslo building
  AI-native applications since 2022."
- Backs claims with concrete numbers (word counts, "the fifth time", reactions).

**Emoji**

- Professional English email: essentially none.
- Warm/known threads: a single ☺️ or 😊 slips in, with a space before it
  ("any input here ☺️", "whenever it fits you 😊").

**Rhythm & punctuation**

- Short declarative sentences; uses very short standalone sentences and one-line
  paragraphs for emphasis: "So the short answer is yes:" / "So my honest view
  is this:".
- Two-option contrast framing when advising: "If your main goal is X... If your
  goal is Y...".
- Colon to set up a list or punchline. Parenthetical asides
  ("(Claude Code, Cursor, etc.)"). Never em dashes.
- Bulleted/numbered lists in longer emails.

**Stance**

- Honest and transparent, even when it costs him: states a refund policy, a
  conflict of interest, an oversight ("You're right, and I'm sorry about
  this."), then offers concrete help.
- Disclaims pitch intent when it could read as one: "This isn't a pitch for
  coverage of my product. It's a tip about a pattern...".
- Closes by handing the next move over: "Let me know if you'd like any changes",
  "Don't hesitate to reach out".
